# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Two audiences with different buying paths:
1. **Working barbers** (the core audience) — pros who cut daily, care about RPM, blade tech, battery life, and weight. They buy at trade price from empirebarber.ca. They watch barber IG, they know Wahl/BabylissPro/JRL, and they judge tools like athletes judge gear.
2. **Home users / gift buyers** — consumers who want pro-grade tools; they buy on Amazon. Less spec-literate, more swayed by look, story, and confidence.

Context of use: mobile-heavy (Instagram traffic from the Spada commercials), evening browsing. The site is the landing point for the brand's spec-commercial campaign.

## Product Purpose

A cinematic multi-page marketing/e-commerce spec site for Spada Barber. It showcases the flagship TORQ collection (Clipper $159.99, Trimmer $139.99, Shaver $89.99 CAD) plus the supporting catalog (WAVE/SONAR blades, foils, razor blades, cam followers). It does not process checkout: every buy action routes to Amazon (consumer) or empirebarber.ca (trade). Success = visitors feel the brand's world, understand the specs quickly, and click through to buy on the right channel.

## Brand Personality

Forged. Gladiatorial. Cinematic. "Spada" is Italian for sword; the commercials cast the barber as a warrior and the tool as a weapon — gladiators, the Colosseum, fog-drenched arenas, engraved steel. Visual signature: steel-blue/teal cinematic grade, white tools under a hard top-spotlight, electric-blue LED glow, aggressive italic chrome SPADA wordmark. Voice: short, declarative, confident. Mythic but never campy; specs stated like weapon stats.

## Anti-references

- Generic Shopify/theme store look (top bar, product card grid, sale badges everywhere).
- The barber-industry default: black-and-gold "bling" branding (BabylissPro Gold, skull graphics, chrome flames).
- SaaS landing-page grammar: eyebrow labels over every section, icon cards in threes, gradient text.
- Bright lifestyle e-commerce (pastel, airy) — this brand lives at night, in the arena.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the commercial** — same grade, same fog, same spotlight staging as the campaign footage; web and film feel like one production.
2. **Specs are weapon stats** — RPM, torque, runtime presented with the gravity of an armory plaque, not a feature list.
3. **One idea per fold** — cinematic pacing; each viewport commits to a single product, claim, or moment.
4. **Two doors, no friction** — every buy moment cleanly forks: pros → Empire Barber trade price, everyone else → Amazon. Never make the user hunt for their door.
5. **White blade in a dark arena** — the white tools are the brightest object on every page; darkness exists to stage them.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 against the dark grounds (fog-blue secondary text tuned for contrast). Full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives (fog drift, light sweeps, and reveals collapse to crossfades/static). Semantic HTML, keyboard-operable buy dialog, alt text written in brand voice.
